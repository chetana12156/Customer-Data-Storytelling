# Customer Segmentation & Marketing Analytics – Data Storytelling & Statistical Validation

## Objective

The objective of this project was to transform analytical findings into a meaningful business narrative and validate key insights using statistical methods. The project combines interactive dashboarding, customer segmentation, marketing analytics, and hypothesis testing to support data-driven decision-making.


## Project Overview

This project focuses on understanding customer behavior, spending patterns, campaign engagement, and customer satisfaction using customer marketing data.

The analysis was summarized through an interactive Tableau dashboard and a stakeholder-focused presentation. Statistical validation was performed to verify whether customer income significantly influences spending behavior.


## Dashboard Components

The interactive dashboard includes:

- Total Customers KPI
- Average Income KPI
- Average Spending KPI
- Response Rate KPI
- Complaint Rate KPI
- Customer Segment Distribution
- Complaint Distribution by Customer Segment
- Income vs Spending Analysis
- Purchase Channel Analysis
- Customer Segment Response Analysis


## Hypothesis Testing

### Null Hypothesis (H₀)
Customer income has no significant effect on total spending.

### Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)
Customer income significantly affects total spending.

### Statistical Test Used
Independent Two-Sample T-Test

### Results
- Median Income: 51,381.5
- T-Statistic: 52.4418
- P-Value: 0.0000

### Conclusion
Since the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis was rejected. The results indicate that higher-income customers spend significantly more than lower-income customers.


## Key Insights

- Income has a strong influence on customer spending behavior.
- Low Value customers form the largest customer segment.
- Store purchases account for the highest purchase activity.
- Web purchases contribute significantly to customer engagement.
- Campaign response rates indicate moderate marketing effectiveness.
- Complaint rates remain extremely low, reflecting positive customer satisfaction.
- Customer segmentation provides opportunities for targeted marketing and customer retention strategies.


## Business Recommendations

- Develop personalized campaigns for different customer segments.
- Focus retention efforts on High Value customers.
- Create upselling strategies for Medium Value customers.
- Strengthen digital marketing and web purchasing channels.
- Continue maintaining customer satisfaction standards.
